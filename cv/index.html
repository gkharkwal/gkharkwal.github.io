Projects Noisy Sentence Processing
For my dissertation, I am investigating how people process sentences that contain unknown words. Through a series of experiments, we are examining the influence of syntactic and morphological information in deciphering novel words. An example demonstrating that we can indeed make some sense out of novel, "nonsense," words is the Jabberwocky.

Semantic parsing using LWFGs
For my M.S. in Computer Science, I worked with Smaranda Muresan and Matthew Stone to develop a semantic parser that used a constraint-based grammatical framework called Lexicalized Well-Founded Grammars. The code for the grammar and the parser was styled after NLTK's modules, and can be accessed here.

Good-enough language processing
As part of my M.S. in Cognitive Psychology, we investigated how people integrate simultaneously presented visual and linguistic information. We used a sentence-video matching task to provide support for the "good-enough" hypothesis of language comprehension, which posits that linguistic representations are not always exact and depend on the nature of the task.

  • Kharkwal, G. and Stromswold, K. (2014)
